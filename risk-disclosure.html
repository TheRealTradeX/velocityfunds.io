Trading in futures, derivatives, and other leveraged financial instruments involves a high degree of risk and is not suitable for all investors. This Risk Disclosure is intended to help you understand the nature of those risks when using Velocity Funds LLC (“Velocity Funds”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) evaluation and simulated funded account programs.

1. Market Risk & Volatility

Futures and derivatives markets can be highly volatile. Price movements may be sudden and substantial, and can be driven by a wide range of factors including economic data, geopolitical events, central bank policies, liquidity conditions, and market sentiment. Even disciplined strategies can experience rapid and significant drawdowns.

2. Leverage & Magnified Losses

Trading futures typically involves leverage, which allows you to control a large notional position with a smaller amount of capital. While leverage can amplify gains, it also magnifies losses. A relatively small move in the underlying market can result in substantial losses and may quickly exceed the value of your account in a live environment.

Even though Velocity Funds operates in a simulated environment, your risk parameters (such as account size, daily loss limits, and trailing drawdown) are designed to reflect the realities of leveraged trading risk and may result in rapid failure of an evaluation or termination of a simulated funded account.

3. Simulated Trading vs. Live Trading

Velocity Funds programs are based on simulated trading. Orders, fills, and price feeds in our environment are not live executions on regulated exchanges. As a result:

  • Simulated fills may differ from real-world fills in terms of slippage, latency, and liquidity.
  • Simulated performance may not accurately reflect emotional and psychological pressures experienced in live trading.
  • Risk controls may behave differently than those of an actual brokerage account.

Past performance in a simulated environment does not guarantee future performance in live markets. No representation is made that any account will achieve profits or losses similar to those seen in a simulation or example.

Trading, whether live or simulated, should only be undertaken with risk capital — funds that you can afford to lose without impacting your financial stability, lifestyle, or obligations.
